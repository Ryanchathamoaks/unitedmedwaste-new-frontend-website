Understanding Evergreen Clauses in Medical Waste Contracts

The evergreen clause is just one of the many things your medical business needs to look out for in your waste contract. Here's a few more!

Back to Insights

If you're entering into a contract with a medical waste management company, it's crucial to be aware of evergreen clauses. These provisions can auto-renew agreements, potentially locking you into long-term contracts.

Why Evergreen Clauses Exist

Busy healthcare facility managers may not always have the time to thoroughly review waste management agreements. Some companies capitalize on this by embedding evergreen clauses, hoping you'll overlook the details. It's essential not to fall into the trap of automatically renewing contracts.

An evergreen clause ensures that an agreement will auto-renew at set intervals (typically annually) unless one party actively cancels it. Essentially, the contract persists indefinitely until someone terminates it.

For instance:

"This agreement shall renew automatically on each anniversary of the effective date for a one (1) year term unless cancelled in writing by either party with 90 days written notice prior to the next renewal date."

These clauses can also be referred to as:

  • Automatic renewal clauses
  • Perpetual clauses
  • Rolling contracts

Their main advantage is offering vendors continuity without the need to renegotiate contracts. However, they can trap customers in potentially disadvantageous agreements.

How to Spot Evergreen Clauses

To ensure you're not inadvertently agreeing to an evergreen clause:

  • Seek out phrases like "automatic renewal"
  • Be wary of perpetuity clauses that make contracts last indefinitely
  • Understand the advance notice period required for cancellation (commonly 60-90 days)
  • Check the start and end dates of the agreement
  • Ensure you have the option to terminate for any reason, not just specific causes

Always read each section thoroughly and avoid rushing through the signing process.

Strategies for Managing Evergreen Clauses

If you find yourself having to agree to an evergreen clause:

  • Negotiate for a shorter renewal period (preferably one year or less)
  • Try to lessen the cancellation notice period
  • Aim for flexibility in termination without incurring penalties
  • Limit any automatic price hike percentages
  • Incorporate language that grants you the choice to renew or terminate at each interval

It's always a good idea to consult with a lawyer when reviewing or amending contract terms.

Outsmart Evergreen Clauses

Although evergreen contracts tend to favor vendors, you can take steps to protect yourself:

✔️ Mark your calendar for timely cancellation notices

✔️ Negotiate at each renewal instead of passively accepting extensions

✔️ Prepare termination letters ahead of time to exercise your right to cancel

✔️ Research competitors and consider switching providers once your contract concludes

✔️ Insist on transparent pricing without hidden fees or increases

By staying vigilant, you can prevent evergreen clauses from automatically renewing unfavorable contracts. Always scrutinize the fine print, negotiate better terms when possible, and be proactive in your cancellation decisions.
