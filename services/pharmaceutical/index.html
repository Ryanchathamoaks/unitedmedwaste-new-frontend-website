Pharmaceutical Waste Disposal FAQs

What is pharmaceutical waste?

Pharmaceutical waste can include expired, unused, damaged, contaminated, or discarded medications, along with certain containers and materials that held medications. Some pharmaceutical waste may need separate handling from regular medical waste.

Can pharmaceutical waste go in red bag waste?

Pharmaceutical waste should not be placed into red bag waste unless the facility has confirmed that this is appropriate for the specific material and applicable rules. Many medication waste streams require separate containers and documentation.

How do facilities separate hazardous and non-hazardous pharmaceutical waste?

Facilities usually start with waste characterization, formulary review, and internal policy. Hazardous pharmaceuticals may fall under RCRA or state hazardous waste rules, so pharmacy, compliance, and legal teams should be involved where needed.

Do clinics and pharmacies need pharmaceutical waste pickup?

Clinics, pharmacies, hospitals, long-term care facilities, and healthcare offices may need pharmaceutical waste pickup when they generate expired, unused, damaged, or otherwise discarded medications.

What records are needed for pharmaceutical waste disposal?

Records may include pickup documentation, waste stream notes, manifests, certificates, or other disposal records depending on the waste type and facility requirements.
