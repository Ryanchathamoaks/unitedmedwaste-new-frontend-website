Sharps Disposal Services FAQs

What is included in a sharps management program?

A sharps management program can include rigid sharps containers, placement guidance, container exchange, pickup scheduling, disposal coordination, and service records for needles, syringes, lancets, scalpels, and similar items.

Where should sharps containers be placed?

Sharps containers should be placed near the point of use so staff can dispose of sharps immediately after use. Placement should also account for patient access, staff workflow, container fill level, and facility policy.

How often should sharps containers be replaced?

Replacement depends on fill level, procedure volume, storage space, and internal policy. Containers should not be overfilled, and staff should follow the fill line and closure instructions for the container used.

Can sharps go in red bag waste?

Loose sharps should not be placed in red bag waste. Used sharps need puncture-resistant sharps containers that are appropriate for the facility's workflow and applicable requirements.

Who needs sharps disposal services?

Clinics, dental offices, hospitals, labs, pharmacies, long-term care facilities, veterinary practices, and other settings that use needles or puncture-capable instruments may need sharps disposal service.
